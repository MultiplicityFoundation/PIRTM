# 🧠 PIRTM Primer

## 📌 Introduction to Prime-Indexed Recursive Tensor Mathematics (PIRTM)

**PIRTM** is a formalism for constructing recursively evolving systems over prime-indexed tensor structures. It integrates number theory, spectral analysis, and quantum feedback to stabilize nonlinear systems that exhibit high-dimensional complexity and recursive depth.

At its core, PIRTM replaces standard indexing and linear time evolution with prime-weighted recursive operators:

\[
\Xi(t+1) = \Lambda_m \cdot \Xi(t) \cdot T_{p_i} + \Phi(t)
\]

Where:
- \( \Xi(t) \): Dynamic recursive operator
- \( \Lambda_m \): Universal Multiplicity Constant
- \( T_{p_i} \): Tensor operator indexed by the \(i\)-th prime
- \( \Phi(t) \): External forcing or entropy vector

This architecture allows for:
- Modular symmetry tracking
- Recursive stabilization
- Eigenstate convergence across quantum-classical boundaries

## 🌊 Prime Eigenvalue Oscillation Hypothesis (PEOH)

PEOH posits that **non-trivial zeros** of the Riemann zeta function \( \zeta(s) \) emerge as **destructive interference patterns** among prime-indexed eigenmodes of a non-linear multiplicative operator \( \mathcal{M} \):

\[
\mathcal{M} \psi_n = p_n \psi_n
\]

This leads to a spectral interference structure:

\[
\Xi_{\zeta}(s) = \sum_{p_i \in \mathbb{P}} \psi_{p_i} e^{-s \log p_i}
\]

PEOH predicts:
- Zeros lie on \( \Re(s) = \frac{1}{2} \) due to critical eigenvalue interference
- Stability is ensured if the recursive coefficient:

\[
k = \sum_{p_i \in P_N} \Lambda_m p_i^\alpha < 1 \quad \text{with } \alpha < -1
\]

- Prime interference encodes fractal standing waves consistent with quantum chaotic behavior
